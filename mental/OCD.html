    • What is Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder?

    Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD) is a chronic mental health condition characterized by a cycle of uncontrollable, recurring thoughts (obsessions) and repetitive behaviors (compulsions). These obsessions cause significant anxiety, leading to time-consuming compulsions performed to temporarily relieve distress.

    Components: It covers emotional (feelings), psychological (thoughts/logic), and social (relationships) health.
    Functionality: Good mental health enables people to realize their abilities, cope with normal life stresses, work productively, and contribute to their communities.

    How can I overcome my OCD?

    Effective treatment for Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD) typically involves a combination of specialized therapy and medication. The "gold standard" approach is Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP), a type of Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) that helps patients confront fears and reduce compulsive behaviors.
