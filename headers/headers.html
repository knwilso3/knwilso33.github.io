Beetles

External Morphlogy

Mouthparts

Beetle mouthparts are mostly for chewing and that includes a labrum, paired mandibles, paired maxille, and a labium. They can also be used in combat.

Thorax

The thorax is the middle part of the beetle where two pairs of wings and 6 legs are attached.

Prothorax and pterothorax

The Prothorax is the first part of the thorax bearing the first pair of legs and a protective shell. The pterothorax is the combined second (mesothorax) and third (metathorax) segments of the thorax, which are enlarged and specialized for flight in winged insects.

Legs

The beetle has 6 legs, they are jointed appendeges with blades on the end for grip.

wings

Beetles have two pairs of wings: a hard, protective forewing called an elytron that acts as an armored shell, and a thinner, more delicate hind wing used for flight that folds underneath the elytra when not in use

Abdomen

Contains the digestive system, Reproductive organs, and respritory stuctures like spiracles..
